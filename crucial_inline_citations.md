### **SECTION 081416: WOOD DOORS - COMPREHENSIVE ANALYSIS**

This specification section details the requirements for various types of wood doors, including solid-core doors with wood-veneer and plastic-laminate faces, factory finishing, factory fitting, and machining for hardware, with a specific mention of "Najdi style Solid Wood Doors." It outlines general provisions, applicable standards, sustainable design criteria, fire and acoustic requirements, product specifications, and installation procedures <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

---

### **PART 1 - GENERAL PROVISIONS**

This section establishes the foundational administrative and procedural requirements applicable to the wood doors scope of work <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.1 RELATED DOCUMENTS & SUMMARY**
The installation and detailing of wood doors must be coordinated with all Contract Documents, including General and Supplementary Conditions <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. This section is particularly linked with Section "018113.19 - Sustainable Design Requirements" <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. The scope covers solid-core doors with various facing materials, factory finishing, precision factory fitting, hardware machining, and the provision of specialized Najdi style Solid Wood Doors <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.2 REFERENCES & STANDARDS**
A wide array of industry standards and association guidelines are stipulated to govern the quality, performance, and construction of wood doors <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. The contractor is required to maintain copies of all referenced standards on site and provide them to the Engineer upon request <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. In case of conflict, the most stringent condition governs <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

Key referenced organizations and standards include:
*   **Window and Door Manufacturers Association (WDMA):** WDMA I.S.6-99, ANSI/WDMA I.S.6A-01, WDMA I.S.4-00, TM-6-88, TM-7-90, TM-8-90, TM-10-90 <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Architectural Woodwork Institute (AWI):** AWI - Quality Standards Illustrated <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **American National Standards Institute (ANSI):** ANSI A135.4, ANSI A 208.2 <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **British Standards (BS):** BS 4787-1, BS EN 14220 & 14221 <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Underwriters Laboratories Inc. (UL):** UL 10B, UL: Building Materials Directory <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **National Electrical Manufacturers Association (NEMA):** NEMA LD 3 <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **National Fire Protection Association (NFPA):** NFPA 80, NFPA 252 <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **SASO Saudi - Arabian Standards Organization:** SASO 472, 1254, 1256, 1414, 1590, 1756, 1757 <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.3 GENERAL REQUIREMENTS & LOCAL CODES**
All work must strictly adhere to the latest Saudi Building Code (SBC-201), Saudi Fire & Life Safety Code of Practice, and Saudi Civil Defence Requirements, alongside other applicable local codes, regulations, and authorities <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.4 SUSTAINABLE DESIGN REQUIREMENTS (LEED & MOSTADAM)**
The project targets LEED Gold BD+C V4 and MOSTADAM COMMERCIAL Gold D+C ratings, requiring the Contractor to comply with USGBC LEED and MOSTADAM Reference Guides <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. The Contractor must submit all necessary sustainability documentation, including Environmental Product Declarations (EPDs), details on raw material sourcing, and information on low-emitting materials <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. In cases of overlap or difference, the more stringent requirements must be followed <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

*   **MOSTADAM CREDIT HC-11 / LEED CREDIT EQC2: VOCs and Low-Emitting Materials:** Adhesives and sealants must meet SCAQMD Rule 1168 and CDPH Standard Method v1.1-201 emissions <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Composite wood materials must not exceed specific formaldehyde emissions (0.05 ppm for hardwood plywood, 0.09 ppm for particleboard, 0.11 ppm for MDF) per CDPH Standard Method v1.1-2010 <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Paints and coatings must not exceed VOC limits of SCAQMD Rule 1113, Green Seal GS-03, and GS-11 <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **MOSTADAM CREDIT MW-05: Sustainable Materials:** At least 50% (by cost) of all timber and composite wood products must be Chain of Custody (CoC) certified <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.5 FIRE AND ACOUSTIC REQUIREMENTS**
This section must be read in conjunction with the project's Fire & Life Safety Report and Acoustic Report <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. The Contractor must submit proposals and test certificates demonstrating compliance, and all requirements from these reports must be met within the contract price <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.6 DEFINITIONS**
*   **Fire Resisting Door:** Constructed and tested to pass NFPA 252 and 10B, rated for a specified fire duration, with an identifying label from an approved independent testing and inspection agency <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Fire Door Assembly:** Encompasses the door frame, door leaf, all hardware, accessories, and vision lites, all labeled and tested as an assembly <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Smoke Door:** A fire-resisting door with intumescent seals for hot smoke and approved draught seals for cold smoke, installed per NFPA 105, and carrying an identifying label <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Direction Of Opening:** Indicated on Architectural Drawings with a dotted triangle whose vertex points to the hinge or pivot side <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.7 PREINSTALLATION MEETINGS**
A pre-installation conference must be conducted at the project site before commencing any work related to wood doors <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.8 ACTION SUBMITTALS**
The Contractor must furnish various submittals for review and approval <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>:
*   **Product Data:** Detailed information for each door type <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Shop Drawings:** Comprehensive drawings indicating door location, size, hand, elevation, construction details, hardware machining, cutouts, undercuts, veneer matching, factory finishing, and fire-protection ratings <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Samples for Initial Selection:** Samples for wood veneer, plastic-laminate door faces, and factory-finished doors <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Samples for Verification:** Factory finishes on actual door face materials (200x250 mm), plastic laminate samples (150 mm square), door corner sections (200x250 mm), and louver/light opening frame sections (150 mm long) <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.9 INFORMATIONAL SUBMITTALS**
The Contractor must provide a sample warranty, AWI Quality Certification Program certificates, and Sound Transmission Class (STC) Test Data <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.10 REGULATORY REQUIREMENTS**
All fire-rated door construction must comply with Authorities Having Jurisdiction (AHJ), UL 10B, and NFPA 252 <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Installation must conform to AHJ, UL 10B, and NFPA 80 <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Fire-rated assemblies must be manufactured per UL requirements and AHJ, bearing appropriate labels <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.11 QUALITY ASSURANCE**
*   **Manufacturer Qualifications:** Manufacturers must be certified for WDMA quality standards and be a certified participant in AWI's Quality Certification Program <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Fire-Rated Doors:** All fire-rated doors in escape corridors must have integrity and insulation (EI) equal to the specified rating <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Mockups:** Mockups are required before installing flush wood doors and frames to verify selections and demonstrate aesthetic quality and execution <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Engineer approval is mandatory before custom fabrication <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.12 DELIVERY, STORAGE, AND HANDLING**
Doors must be handled and stored according to referenced standards and manufacturer's instructions <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Each door must be individually packaged, wrapped, and marked with the corresponding opening number <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.13 FIELD CONDITIONS**
Doors should not be delivered or installed until spaces are enclosed, weathertight, wet work is complete and dry, and the HVAC system is operational, maintaining ambient temperature between 16 and 32 degrees C and relative humidity between 17 and 50 percent <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **1.14 WARRANTY**
A comprehensive warranty is required, making the Manufacturer, Contractor, and Installer jointly liable for defective doors <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Failures include warping exceeding 6.4 mm in a 1067x2134 mm section, or telegraphing of core construction exceeding 0.25 mm in a 76.2 mm span <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. The warranty period for solid-core exterior doors is five years from Substantial Completion, and for solid-core interior doors, it is ten years <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

### **PART 2 - PRODUCTS**

This part details the specific material, construction, and finish requirements for various types of wood doors and related components <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **2.1 FLUSH WOOD DOORS, GENERAL**
All flush wood doors must adhere to WDMA I.S.1-A, "Architectural Wood Flush Doors," and carry AWI Quality Certification Labels <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **WDMA I.S.1-A Performance Grade:** Heavy Duty (unless otherwise indicated), Extra Heavy Duty (for public toilets, janitor's closets, exits), and Standard Duty (for closets, private toilets) <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **2.2 FIRE-RESISTANCE RATED DOOR ASSEMBLIES**
Fire-resisting door assemblies must comply with NFPA 80, be listed and labeled by a qualified testing agency for fire-protection ratings (NFPA 252 or UL 10B), and approved by AHJ <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **General Construction:** AWI Quality Standard, Section 9, Type FD Door, minimum 45mm thickness <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Core:** Noncombustible mineral product <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Stiles & Rails:** Stiles reinforced for full mortise hinges <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Top and bottom rails are Structural Composite Lumber (SCL) or UL-approved composite material <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Edge Construction:** Must include concealed intumescent seals <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Hinge stiles must have improved screw-holding capability (2440 N per WDMA T.M.-10) <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Pairs:** Require formed-steel edges and astragals with intumescent seals, finished with baked enamel <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Adhesives:** Type 1 per WDMA T.M. 6, urea formaldehyde-free <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Door Facing Finish:** Veneered doors (Grade AA AWI, 0.6mm minimum thick) <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>, Plastic Laminated doors (high-pressure laminate, 1.25mm thick, NEMA Standard LD-3 compliant) <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>, or Opaque Finish doors (B-grade Medium Density Overlay (MDO) with WDMA OP-4 or OP-6 finish) <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **2.3 NON-FIRE-RATED SOLID CORE DOORS**
These doors follow AWI Quality Standard, Section 9, Type FD, with a minimum thickness of 45mm <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Core Construction:** Structural-Composite-Lumber-Core (SCL), WDMA I.S.10, five-ply construction <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Screw Withdrawal:** Face withdrawal of 3100 N, edge withdrawal of 1780 N <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Stiles & Rails:** Stiles reinforced for full mortise hinges <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Top and bottom rails are Structural Composite Lumber (SCL) <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Edge Construction:** Hinge stiles with improved screw-holding capability (2440 N per WDMA T.M.-10) <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Adhesives:** Type 1 per WDMA T.M. 6, urea formaldehyde-free <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Door Facing Finish:** Same specifications as Fire-Resistance Rated Door Assemblies <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **2.4 ACOUSTIC RATED DOORS**
Door and frame assemblies must be manufactured to an STC rating measured in accordance with ASTM E90, as specified in the Acoustic Report <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. They must achieve specified STC and fire performance ratings, with suitable thickness, design, and core <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Reinforcement for surface-mounted hardware and machining for mortised hardware is required <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Double doors require metal acoustic astragals with integral acoustic seals <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **2.5 SOLID WOOD DOORS**
Custom fabricated "Najdi style" solid wood doors must match patterns and styles on drawings <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Thickness:** Minimum 45mm for doors up to 2.5m high, and 55mm for doors between 2.5m and 3m <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Wood Species:** Same as standard Najdi style doors, approved in writing by the Engineer, and conforming to premium grade AWI and/or WDMA <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Exterior Fire-Rated Doors:** Must have a non-combustible core with Najdi style solid wood panel facings <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Compliance:** With BS EN 14220, BS 644, BS 6375-1 where applicable <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Finish:** WDMA OP-4 conversion varnish or WDMA OP-6 catalyzed polyurethane, color selected by the Engineer <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **2.6 DOOR FRAMES**
*   **Wood Frames:** Comply with AWI Section 9, made of solid wood, minimum 45mm thick, with an EPDM bubble gasket and architrave <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Najdi style doors must have matching door frames <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Metal Frames:** Refer to Section 081113 "Hollow Metal Doors and Frames" <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Fire-Rated Frames:** Must be fire-rated as indicated <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Joints and Seals:** Frames must have mitered joints with neoprene or silicone gaskets <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **2.7 LOUVERS**
Metal louvers must have vision-proof, inverted V or Y blades, made of hot-dip galvanized steel (1.0 mm thick), factory primed <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **2.8 FABRICATION**
Doors must be factory fitted to indicated frame-opening sizes, complying with quality standard clearances and NFPA 80 requirements for fire-rated doors <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Hardware Machining:** Factory machine doors for non-surface applied hardware per DHI-WDHS-3, hardware schedules, and templates <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Transom and Side Panels:** Fabricate matching panels with identical construction, surfaces, and finish <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Openings:** Factory cut and trim openings for lights and louvers <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Glazing for factory-finished doors must be factory installed per Section 088000 "Glazing" <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Exterior Doors:** Require water repellent treatment after fabrication and before finishing <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Out-swinging doors must be flashed with manufacturer's standard metal flashing <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **2.9 FACTORY FINISHING**
All factory finishing must comply with referenced quality standards <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Fabrication must be completed before finishing <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. All faces, four edges, edges of cutouts, and mortises must be finished, though stains and fillers may be omitted on specific edges and cutouts <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

### **PART 3 - EXECUTION**

This section outlines the requirements for examination, installation, and adjustment of wood doors <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **3.1 EXAMINATION**
Prior to hanging doors, the Installer, in the presence of the Engineer, must examine doors and installed door frames to verify compliance with type, size, location, swing, and ensure frames have level heads and plumb jambs <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Doors with defects must be rejected, and unsatisfactory conditions corrected before installation proceeds <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **3.2 INSTALLATION**
Installation must strictly adhere to the manufacturer's written instructions and referenced quality standards <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Hardware:** Installation details for hardware are specified in Section 087111 "Door Hardware (Descriptive Specification)" <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Fire-Rated and Smoke-Control Doors:** Must be installed in accordance with NFPA 80 and NFPA 105, respectively <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Job-Fitted Doors:** Doors must be aligned and fitted in frames with uniform clearances and bevels, without trimming stiles and rails beyond manufacturer's limits or fire-rated door allowances <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Edges, cutouts, and mortises must be sealed after fitting and machining <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
    *   **Clearances:** 3.2 mm at heads, jambs, and between pairs of doors <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. 3.2 mm from bottom of door to top of decorative floor finish, or 6.4 mm to top of threshold <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Fire-rated doors must comply with NFPA 80 clearances <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
    *   **Bevels:** Non-fire-rated doors require a 3-1/2 degree bevel at lock and hinge edges <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Fire-rated doors require a 3-1/2 degree bevel at the lock edge <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Factory-Fitted and Factory-Finished Doors:** Factory-fitted doors are to be aligned for uniform clearance <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Any finish restoration required due to onsite fitting must be completed before installation <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

#### **3.3 ADJUSTING**
After installation, doors must be adjusted to ensure proper operation <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Doors that do not swing or operate freely must be rehanged or replaced <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>. Damaged doors or those not complying with requirements must be replaced; repair or refinishing is only permissible if the work complies with all requirements and shows no evidence of repair <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

### **1.11 QUALITY ASSURANCE**

This subsection outlines the stringent quality control measures and qualifications required for the wood doors <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.

*   **Manufacturer Qualifications:** The manufacturers of wood doors must be certified according to WDMA quality standards. Additionally, they must be a certified participant in the Architectural Woodwork Institute's (AWI) Quality Certification Program <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Fire-Rated Doors:** All fire-rated doors situated in escape corridors are mandated to possess an integrity and insulation (EI) rating that is equivalent to the specified fire rating <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
*   **Mockups:** Before the installation of flush wood doors and frames, mockups are a prerequisite. These mockups serve to verify selections and to demonstrate the aesthetic quality and the execution of the work. Approval from the Engineer is compulsory before any custom fabrication can proceed <sup class="citation-marker" data-citation-index="0">1</sup>.
