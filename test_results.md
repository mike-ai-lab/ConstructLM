#### **1.6 DEFINITIONS**
Key terms related to door performance and construction are precisely defined:
*   **Fire Resisting Door:** A door constructed and tested to pass NFPA 252 and 10B, rated for a specified fire duration, and carrying an identifying label from an approved independent testing and inspection agency.
*   **Fire Door Assembly:** Encompasses the door frame, door leaf, all hardware, accessories, and vision lites, all of which must be labeled and tested as an assembly.
*   **Smoke Door:** A fire-resisting door with intumescent seals for hot smoke and approved draught seals for cold smoke, installed per NFPA 105, and carrying an identifying label from an approved independent testing and inspection agency.
*   **Direction Of Opening:** Indicated on Architectural Drawings; hinged or pivoting doors are marked with a dotted triangle whose vertex points to the hinge or pivot side.

#### **1.7 PREINSTALLATION MEETINGS**
A pre-installation conference must be conducted at the project site prior to commencing any work related to wood doors.

#### **1.8 ACTION SUBMITTALS**
The Contractor is required to furnish various submittals for review and approval:
*   **Product Data:** Detailed information for each door type, including core, edge construction, louvers, trim for openings, and factory-finishing specifications.
*   **Shop Drawings:** Comprehensive drawings indicating door location, size, hand, and elevation. These must include construction details not covered in product data, dimensions/locations of blocking, mortises/holes for hardware, cutouts, undercuts, veneer matching requirements, factory finishing details, and fire-protection ratings for fire-rated doors.
*   **Samples for Initial Selection:** Samples for wood veneer, plastic-laminate door faces, and factory-finished doors to establish aesthetic direction.
*   **Samples for Verification:**
    *   Factory finishes on actual door face materials (200x250 mm), including sets of three samples for each wood species and transparent finish showing typical color/grain range.
    *   Plastic laminate samples (150 mm square) for each color, texture, and pattern.
    *   Door corner sections (200x250 mm) representing actual face materials and edges, with samples for each veneer species, solid lumber, plastic laminate color/texture/pattern, and finished veneer-faced doors.
    *   Louver blade and frame sections (150 mm long) for each material and finish.
    *   Frames for light openings (150 mm long) for each material, type, and finish.

#### **1.9 INFORMATIONAL SUBMITTALS**
In addition to action submittals, the Contractor must provide:
*   **Sample Warranty:** For any special warranty specified.
*   **Quality Standard Compliance Certificates:** AWI Quality Certification Program certificates.
*   **Sound Transmission Class (STC) Test Data:** Indicating compliance with STC requirements, including laboratory name, test report number, and date of test.

#### **1.10 REGULATORY REQUIREMENTS**
All fire-rated door construction must comply with the Authorities Having Jurisdiction, UL 10B, and NFPA 252. Installation of fire-rated doors must conform to AHJ, UL 10B, and NFPA 80. Fire-rated assemblies must be manufactured in accordance with Underwriters Laboratories requirements and AHJ and bear appropriate labels. No product shall be manufactured before the approved hardware schedule and templates are received.

#### **1.11 QUALITY ASSURANCE**
Strict quality assurance measures are in place to ensure compliance:
*   **Manufacturer Qualifications:** Manufacturers must be certified for WDMA quality standards and be a certified participant in AWI's Quality Certification Program.
*   **Vendor Qualifications:** Vendors must be certified by the manufacturer.
*   **Fire-Rated Doors:** All fire-rated doors in escape corridors must have integrity and insulation (EI) equal to the specified rating.
*   **Mockups:** Before installing flush wood doors and frames, mockups are required to verify selections and demonstrate aesthetic quality and execution. Mockups, constructed at a location directed by the Engineer for each door type, frame, hardware, and anchorage component, require seven days' prior notification for construction. Engineer approval is mandatory before custom fabrication, and such approval does not validate deviations unless specifically approved in writing. Approved mockups may become part of the completed work if undisturbed; otherwise, they must be removed.

#### **1.12 DELIVERY, STORAGE, AND HANDLING**
Doors must be handled and stored according to referenced standards and manufacturer's instructions. Each door must be individually packaged in cardboard cartons and wrapped in plastic sheeting, and marked on the top and bottom rails with the corresponding opening number from the Shop Drawings.

#### **1.13 FIELD CONDITIONS**
Environmental conditions are critical for door installation. Doors should not be delivered or installed until spaces are enclosed and weathertight, wet work is complete and dry, and the HVAC system is operational, maintaining ambient temperature between 16 and 32 degrees C and relative humidity between 17 and 50 percent throughout the remainder of the construction period.

#### **1.14 WARRANTY**
A comprehensive warranty is required, making the Manufacturer, Contractor, and Installer jointly liable for defective doors that fail in materials or workmanship within the specified warranty period. Failures include warping exceeding 6.4 mm in a 1067x2134 mm section, or telegraphing of core construction in face veneers exceeding 0.25 mm in a 76.2 mm span. The warranty also covers installation and finishing required due to repair or replacement. The warranty period for solid-core exterior doors is five years from Substantial Completion, and for solid-core interior doors, it is ten years.

### **PART 2 - PRODUCTS**

This part details the specific material, construction, and finish requirements for various types of wood doors and related components.

#### **2.1 FLUSH WOOD DOORS, GENERAL**
All flush wood doors must adhere to WDMA I.S.1-A, "Architectural Wood Flush Doors," and carry AWI Quality Certification Labels. Doors must comply with the specified selections from the quality standard and any additional requirements beyond it.
*   **WDMA I.S.1-A Performance Grade:**
    *   **Heavy Duty:** Unless otherwise indicated.
    *   **Extra Heavy Duty:** Required for public toilets, janitor's closets, exits, and other indicated areas.
    *   **Standard Duty:** For closets (excluding janitor's closets), private toilets, and other indicated areas.

#### **2.2 FIRE-RESISTANCE RATED DOOR ASSEMBLIES**
Fire-resisting door assemblies, as shown on drawings, must comply with NFPA 80, be listed and labeled by a qualified testing agency for fire-protection ratings (NFPA 252 or UL 10B), and approved by Authorities Having Jurisdiction.
*   **General Construction:** AWI Quality Standard, Section 9, Type FD Door, with a minimum thickness of 45mm, or as required by local fire departments.
*   **Oversize Assemblies:** Units exceeding tested assembly sizes require certification from a qualified testing agency.
*   **Smoke- and Draft-Control Door Assemblies:** Must be listed and labeled by a qualified testing agency (UL 1784) and installed per NFPA 105.
*   **Mineral Core:** Noncombustible mineral product meeting quality standard requirements and testing agency for fire-protection ratings.
*   **Stiles:** Reinforced to receive full mortise hinges, suited to the specified rating.
*   **Top and Bottom Rails:** Structural Composite Lumber (SCL) or UL-approved composite material to meet label requirements.
*   **Blocking:** Composite blocking with improved screw-holding capability for indoor use, at fire ratings indicated: 125mm top/bottom rail blocking, 125mm mid-rail blocking for exit devices, 115mm minimum net thickness on latch side, and 40mm minimum net thickness on hinge side (after trimming).
*   **Edge Construction:** Must include intumescent seals concealed by outer stile, complying with exposed edge requirements. Hinge stiles must have improved screw-holding capability and split resistance, with a screw-holding capability of 2440 N per WDMA T.M.-10.
*   **Pairs:** Require formed-steel edges and astragals with intumescent seals, finished with baked enamel matching the door color.
*   **Adhesives:** Type 1 per WDMA T.M. 6 for both face and core assembly, with resins complying with ANSI A208.1, and must be urea formaldehyde-free.
*   **Door Facing Finish:**
    *   **Veneered Doors:** Grade AA AWI, 0.6mm minimum thick, conforming to WDMA I.S.1 series. Species, cut, and matching to be approved by the Engineer/Engineer's Representative, with exposed vertical and top edges matching faces.
    *   **Plastic Laminated Doors:** High-pressure laminate, 1.25mm thick, complying with NEMA Standard LD-3. Colors, patterns, and finishes selected by the Engineer/Engineer's Representative. Exposed vertical and top edges can be matching plastic laminate (applied before faces) or impact-resistant polymer edging (applied after faces), as selected and approved.
    *   **Opaque Finish Doors:** Faces of regular B-grade Medium Density Overlay (MDO). Finish: WDMA OP-4 conversion varnish or WDMA OP-6 catalyzed polyurethane, color selected by Engineer, with exposed vertical and top edges of any closed-grain hardwood.

#### **2.3 NON-FIRE-RATED SOLID CORE DOORS**
These doors follow AWI Quality Standard, Section 9, Type FD, with a minimum thickness of 45mm, unless otherwise indicated.
*   **Core Construction:** Structural-Composite-Lumber-Core (SCL), WDMA I.S.10, five-ply construction.
*   **Screw Withdrawal:** Face withdrawal of 3100 N, edge withdrawal of 1780 N.
*   **Stiles:** Reinforced for full mortise hinges.
*   **Top and Bottom Rails:** Structural Composite Lumber (SCL).
*   **Edge Construction:** Hinge stiles with improved screw-holding capability and split resistance, screw-holding capability of 2440 N per WDMA T.M.-10.
*   **Adhesives:** Type 1 per WDMA T.M. 6, ANSI A208.1 compliant, urea formaldehyde-free.
*   **Door Facing Finish:** Same specifications as Fire-Resistance Rated Door Assemblies (Veneered, Plastic Laminated, and Opaque Finish options).

#### **2.4 ACOUSTIC RATED DOORS**
Door and frame assemblies must be manufactured to an STC rating measured in accordance with ASTM E90, as specified in the Acoustic Report.
*   **Fabrication:** To ANSI/WDMA IS1A standards, with suitable thickness, design, and core to achieve specified STC and fire performance ratings.
*   **Reinforcement:** Provided where surface-mounted hardware is required.
*   **Machining:** Drill and tap for mortised, templated hardware.
*   **Top and Bottom Rails:** Factory sealed with wood sealer.
*   **Astragals:** Metal acoustic astragals with integral acoustic seals for double doors.

#### **2.5 SOLID WOOD DOORS**
Custom fabricated solid wood doors, specifically "Najdi style" where indicated, must match patterns and styles on drawings.
*   **Thickness:** Minimum 45mm for doors up to 2.5m high, and 55mm for doors between 2.5m and 3m.
*   **Wood Species:** Must be the same as standard Najdi style doors, approved in writing by the Engineer, and conform to premium grade AWI and/or WDMA.
*   **Carving and Patterns:** Any carving or patterns on wood require approval on shop drawings.
*   **Exterior Fire-Rated Doors:** Must have a non-combustible core (as specified for fire-resistance rated doors) with Najdi style solid wood panel facings on both sides.
*   **Compliance:** With BS EN 14220, BS 644, BS 6375-1 where applicable and not in conflict with specified American codes.
*   **Finish:** Unless otherwise indicated, must match approved samples, with WDMA OP-4 conversion varnish or WDMA OP-6 catalyzed polyurethane, and color selected by the Engineer.

#### **2.6 DOOR FRAMES**
Door frames must be constructed to specific standards and materials.
*   **Wood Frames:** Comply with AWI Section 9, made of solid wood, minimum 45mm thick, with width as shown on drawings, equipped with an EPDM bubble gasket and architrave.
*   **Matching:** Solid wood doors with Najdi style leaves must have matching door frames.
*   **Metal Frames:** Refer to Section 081113 "Hollow Metal Doors and Frames."
*   **Fire-Rated Frames:** Must be fire-rated, as indicated on the Door Schedule.
*   **Joints and Seals:** Frames must have mitered joints with neoprene or silicone gaskets inserted in an integral kerf (groove), or in an aluminum profile fixed to the frame, all approved by the Engineer for weather/acoustic/smoke sealing.

#### **2.7 LOUVERS**
Metal louvers specified for doors must meet the following:
*   **Blade Type:** Vision-proof, inverted V or Y.
*   **Material and Finish:** Hot-dip galvanized steel, 1.0 mm thick, factory primed for a paint finish.

#### **2.8 FABRICATION**
Doors must be factory fitted to indicated frame-opening sizes, complying with referenced quality standard clearances and NFPA 80 requirements for fire-rated doors.
*   **Hardware Machining:** Factory machine doors for non-surface applied hardware, locating hardware per DHI-WDHS-3, hardware schedules, and templates. This requires coordination with hardware mortises in metal frames before factory machining. Metal astragals and formed-steel edges for pairs of fire-rated doors must also be factory machined. Hardware approval must be coordinated with the Fire Alarm System and ADA where necessary.
*   **Transom and Side Panels:** Fabricate matching panels with identical construction, exposed surfaces, and finish as associated doors. Bottom edges of transoms and top edges of rabbeted doors must be finished like door stiles. Panels for doors and transoms must have full-width, solid-lumber, rabbeted, meeting rails, and factory-installed spring bolts for concealed attachment into metal door frame jambs.
*   **Openings:** Factory cut and trim openings through doors. Light openings must be trimmed with moldings of the indicated material and profile. Glazing for factory-finished doors must be factory installed, adhering to Section 088000 "Glazing." Louvers must also be factory installed in prepared openings.
*   **Exterior Doors:** Exterior doors require a water repellent treatment after fabrication but before factory finishing. Top of out-swinging doors must be flashed with the manufacturer's standard metal flashing.

#### **2.9 FACTORY FINISHING**
All factory finishing must comply with referenced quality standards. Fabrication, including fitting doors to openings and machining for hardware, must be completed before finishing.
*   **Scope:** All faces, four edges, edges of cutouts, and mortises must be finished. Stains and fillers may be omitted on top and bottom edges, edges of cutouts, and mortises. Factory finishing is required for doors indicated to receive transparent finish or specified in schedules/drawings.

### **PART 3 - EXECUTION**

This section outlines the requirements for examination, installation, and adjustment of wood doors.

#### **3.1 EXAMINATION**
Prior to hanging doors, the Installer, in the presence of the Engineer, must examine doors and installed door frames to verify compliance with type, size, location, swing characteristics, and ensure frames have level heads and plumb jambs. Doors with defects must be rejected. Installation work shall only proceed after all unsatisfactory conditions have been corrected.

#### **3.2 INSTALLATION**
Installation must strictly adhere to the manufacturer's written instructions and referenced quality standards.
*   **Hardware:** Installation details for hardware are specified in Section 087111 "Door Hardware (Descriptive Specification)."
*   **Fire-Rated and Smoke-Control Doors:** Must be installed in accordance with NFPA 80 and NFPA 105, respectively.
*   **Job-Fitted Doors:** Doors must be aligned and fitted in frames with uniform clearances and bevels, without trimming stiles and rails beyond manufacturer's limits or fire-rated door allowances. Doors must be machined for hardware, and all edges, cutouts, and mortises sealed after fitting and machining.
    *   **Clearances:** 3.2 mm at heads, jambs, and between pairs of doors. 3.2 mm from the bottom of the door to the top of decorative floor finish or covering, or 6.4 mm from the bottom of the door to the top of the threshold where shown or scheduled. Fire-rated doors must comply with NFPA 80 for clearances.
    *   **Bevels:** Non-fire-rated doors require a 3-1/2 degree bevel at lock and hinge edges. Fire-rated doors require a 3-1/2 degree bevel at the lock edge, with stiles and rails trimmed only to the extent permitted by the labeling agency.
*   **Factory-Fitted and Factory-Finished Doors:** Factory-fitted doors are to be aligned for uniform clearance at each edge. For factory-finished doors, any finish restoration required due to onsite fitting or machining must be completed before installation.

#### **3.3 ADJUSTING**
After installation, doors must be adjusted to ensure proper operation.
*   **Operation:** Doors that do not swing or operate freely must be rehanged or replaced.
*   **Finished Doors:** Any damaged doors or those not complying with requirements must be replaced. Repair or refinishing is permissible only if the work complies with all requirements and shows no evidence of repair or refinishing.
