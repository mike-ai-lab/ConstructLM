SECTION 081416: WOOD DOORS - COMPREHENSIVE ANALYSIS
This specification section details the requirements for various types of wood doors, including solid-core doors with wood-veneer and plastic-laminate faces, factory finishing, factory fitting, and machining for hardware, with a specific mention of "Najdi style Solid Wood Doors." It outlines general provisions, applicable standards, sustainable design criteria, fire and acoustic requirements, product specifications, and installation procedures 1.

PART 1 - GENERAL PROVISIONS
This section establishes the foundational administrative and procedural requirements applicable to the wood doors scope of work 1.
1.1 RELATED DOCUMENTS & SUMMARY
The installation and detailing of wood doors must be coordinated with all Contract Documents, including General and Supplementary Conditions 1. This section is particularly linked with Section "018113.19 - Sustainable Design Requirements" 1. The scope covers solid-core doors with various facing materials, factory finishing, precision factory fitting, hardware machining, and the provision of specialized Najdi style Solid Wood Doors 1.
1.2 REFERENCES & STANDARDS
A wide array of industry standards and association guidelines are stipulated to govern the quality, performance, and construction of wood doors 1. The contractor is required to maintain copies of all referenced standards on site and provide them to the Engineer upon request 1. In case of conflict, the most stringent condition governs 1.
Key referenced organizations and standards include:

Window and Door Manufacturers Association (WDMA): WDMA I.S.6-99, ANSI/WDMA I.S.6A-01, WDMA I.S.4-00, TM-6-88, TM-7-90, TM-8-90, TM-10-90 1.
Architectural Woodwork Institute (AWI): AWI - Quality Standards Illustrated 1.
American National Standards Institute (ANSI): ANSI A135.4, ANSI A 208.2 1.
British Standards (BS): BS 4787-1, BS EN 14220 & 14221 1.
Underwriters Laboratories Inc. (UL): UL 10B, UL: Building Materials Directory 1.
National Electrical Manufacturers Association (NEMA): NEMA LD 3 1.
National Fire Protection Association (NFPA): NFPA 80, NFPA 252 1.
SASO Saudi - Arabian Standards Organization: SASO 472, 1254, 1256, 1414, 1590, 1756, 1757 1.

1.3 GENERAL REQUIREMENTS & LOCAL CODES
All work must strictly adhere to the latest Saudi Building Code (SBC-201), Saudi Fire & Life Safety Code of Practice, and Saudi Civil Defence Requirements, alongside other applicable local codes, regulations, and authorities 1.
1.4 SUSTAINABLE DESIGN REQUIREMENTS (LEED & MOSTADAM)
The project targets LEED Gold BD+C V4 and MOSTADAM COMMERCIAL Gold D+C ratings, requiring the Contractor to comply with USGBC LEED and MOSTADAM Reference Guides 1. The Contractor must submit all necessary sustainability documentation, including Environmental Product Declarations (EPDs), details on raw material sourcing, and information on low-emitting materials 1. In cases of overlap or difference, the more stringent requirements must be followed 1.

MOSTADAM CREDIT HC-11 / LEED CREDIT EQC2: VOCs and Low-Emitting Materials: Adhesives and sealants must meet SCAQMD Rule 1168 and CDPH Standard Method v1.1-201 emissions 1. Composite wood materials must not exceed specific formaldehyde emissions (0.05 ppm for hardwood plywood, 0.09 ppm for particleboard, 0.11 ppm for MDF) per CDPH Standard Method v1.1-2010 1. Paints and coatings must not exceed VOC limits of SCAQMD Rule 1113, Green Seal GS-03, and GS-11 1.
MOSTADAM CREDIT MW-05: Sustainable Materials: At least 50% (by cost) of all timber and composite wood products must be Chain of Custody (CoC) certified 1.

1.5 FIRE AND ACOUSTIC REQUIREMENTS
This section must be read in conjunction with the project's Fire & Life Safety Report and Acoustic Report 1. The Contractor must submit proposals and test certificates demonstrating compliance, and all requirements from these reports must be met within the contract price 1.
1.6 DEFINITIONS

Fire Resisting Door: Constructed and tested to pass NFPA 252 and 10B, rated for a specified fire duration, with an identifying label from an approved independent testing and inspection agency 1.
Fire Door Assembly: Encompasses the door frame, door leaf, all hardware, accessories, and vision lites, all labeled and tested as an assembly 1.
Smoke Door: A fire-resisting door with intumescent seals for hot smoke and approved draught seals for cold smoke, installed per NFPA 105, and carrying an identifying label 1.
Direction Of Opening: Indicated on Architectural Drawings with a dotted triangle whose vertex points to the hinge or pivot side 1.

1.7 PREINSTALLATION MEETINGS
A pre-installation conference must be conducted at the project site before commencing any work related to wood doors 1.
1.8 ACTION SUBMITTALS
The Contractor must furnish various submittals for review and approval 1:

Product Data: Detailed information for each door type 1.
Shop Drawings: Comprehensive drawings indicating door location, size, hand, elevation, construction details, hardware machining, cutouts, undercuts, veneer matching, factory finishing, and fire-protection ratings 1.
Samples for Initial Selection: Samples for wood veneer, plastic-laminate door faces, and factory-finished doors 1.
Samples for Verification: Factory finishes on actual door face materials (200x250 mm), plastic laminate samples (150 mm square), door corner sections (200x250 mm), and louver/light opening frame sections (150 mm long) 1.

1.9 INFORMATIONAL SUBMITTALS
The Contractor must provide a sample warranty, AWI Quality Certification Program certificates, and Sound Transmission Class (STC) Test Data 1.
1.10 REGULATORY REQUIREMENTS
All fire-rated door construction must comply with Authorities Having Jurisdiction (AHJ), UL 10B, and NFPA 252 1. Installation must conform to AHJ, UL 10B, and NFPA 80 1. Fire-rated assemblies must be manufactured per UL requirements and AHJ, bearing appropriate labels 1.
1.11 QUALITY ASSURANCE

Manufacturer Qualifications: Manufacturers must be certified for WDMA quality standards and be a certified participant in AWI's Quality Certification Program 1.
Fire-Rated Doors: All fire-rated doors in escape corridors must have integrity and insulation (EI) equal to the specified rating 1.
Mockups: Mockups are required before installing flush wood doors and frames to verify selections and demonstrate aesthetic quality and execution 1. Engineer approval is mandatory before custom fabrication 1.

1.12 DELIVERY, STORAGE, AND HANDLING
Doors must be handled and stored according to referenced standards and manufacturer's instructions 1. Each door must be individually packaged, wrapped, and marked with the corresponding opening number 1.
1.13 FIELD CONDITIONS
Doors should not be delivered or installed until spaces are enclosed, weathertight, wet work is complete and dry, and the HVAC system is operational, maintaining ambient temperature between 16 and 32 degrees C and relative humidity between 17 and 50 percent 1.
1.14 WARRANTY
A comprehensive warranty is required, making the Manufacturer, Contractor, and Installer jointly liable for defective doors 1. Failures include warping exceeding 6.4 mm in a 1067x2134 mm section, or telegraphing of core construction exceeding 0.25 mm in a 76.2 mm span 1. The warranty period for solid-core exterior doors is five years from Substantial Completion, and for solid-core interior doors, it is ten years 1.

PART 2 - PRODUCTS
This part details the specific material, construction, and finish requirements for various types of wood doors and related components 1.
2.1 FLUSH WOOD DOORS, GENERAL
All flush wood doors must adhere to WDMA I.S.1-A, "Architectural Wood Flush Doors," and carry AWI Quality Certification Labels 1.

WDMA I.S.1-A Performance Grade: Heavy Duty (unless otherwise indicated), Extra Heavy Duty (for public toilets, janitor's closets, exits), and Standard Duty (for closets, private toilets) 1.

2.2 FIRE-RESISTANCE RATED DOOR ASSEMBLIES
Fire-resisting door assemblies must comply with NFPA 80, be listed and labeled by a qualified testing agency for fire-protection ratings (NFPA 252 or UL 10B), and approved by AHJ 1.

General Construction: AWI Quality Standard, Section 9, Type FD Door, minimum 45mm thickness 1.
Core: Noncombustible mineral product 1.
Stiles & Rails: Stiles reinforced for full mortise hinges 1. Top and bottom rails are Structural Composite Lumber (SCL) or UL-approved composite material 1.
Edge Construction: Must include concealed intumescent seals 1. Hinge stiles must have improved screw-holding capability (2440 N per WDMA T.M.-10) 1.
Pairs: Require formed-steel edges and astragals with intumescent seals, finished with baked enamel 1.
Adhesives: Type 1 per WDMA T.M. 6, urea formaldehyde-free 1.
Door Facing Finish: Veneered doors (Grade AA AWI, 0.6mm minimum thick) 1, Plastic Laminated doors (high-pressure laminate, 1.25mm thick, NEMA Standard LD-3 compliant) 1, or Opaque Finish doors (B-grade Medium Density Overlay (MDO) with WDMA OP-4 or OP-6 finish) 1.

2.3 NON-FIRE-RATED SOLID CORE DOORS
These doors follow AWI Quality Standard, Section 9, Type FD, with a minimum thickness of 45mm 1.

Core Construction: Structural-Composite-Lumber-Core (SCL), WDMA I.S.10, five-ply construction 1.
Screw Withdrawal: Face withdrawal of 3100 N, edge withdrawal of 1780 N 1.
Stiles & Rails: Stiles reinforced for full mortise hinges 1. Top and bottom rails are Structural Composite Lumber (SCL) 1.
Edge Construction: Hinge stiles with improved screw-holding capability (2440 N per WDMA T.M.-10) 1.
Adhesives: Type 1 per WDMA T.M. 6, urea formaldehyde-free 1.
Door Facing Finish: Same specifications as Fire-Resistance Rated Door Assemblies 1.

2.4 ACOUSTIC RATED DOORS
Door and frame assemblies must be manufactured to an STC rating measured in accordance with ASTM E90, as specified in the Acoustic Report 1. They must achieve specified STC and fire performance ratings, with suitable thickness, design, and core 1. Reinforcement for surface-mounted hardware and machining for mortised hardware is required 1. Double doors require metal acoustic astragals with integral acoustic seals 1.
2.5 SOLID WOOD DOORS
Custom fabricated "Najdi style" solid wood doors must match patterns and styles on drawings 1.

Thickness: Minimum 45mm for doors up to 2.5m high, and 55mm for doors between 2.5m and 3m 1.
Wood Species: Same as standard Najdi style doors, approved in writing by the Engineer, and conforming to premium grade AWI and/or WDMA 1.
Exterior Fire-Rated Doors: Must have a non-combustible core with Najdi style solid wood panel facings 1.
Compliance: With BS EN 14220, BS 644, BS 6375-1 where applicable 1.
Finish: WDMA OP-4 conversion varnish or WDMA OP-6 catalyzed polyurethane, color selected by the Engineer 1.

2.6 DOOR FRAMES

Wood Frames: Comply with AWI Section 9, made of solid wood, minimum 45mm thick, with an EPDM bubble gasket and architrave 1. Najdi style doors must have matching door frames 1.
Metal Frames: Refer to Section 081113 "Hollow Metal Doors and Frames" 1.
Fire-Rated Frames: Must be fire-rated as indicated 1.
Joints and Seals: Frames must have mitered joints with neoprene or silicone gaskets 1.

2.7 LOUVERS
Metal louvers must have vision-proof, inverted V or Y blades, made of hot-dip galvanized steel (1.0 mm thick), factory primed 1.
2.8 FABRICATION
Doors must be factory fitted to indicated frame-opening sizes, complying with quality standard clearances and NFPA 80 requirements for fire-rated doors 1.

Hardware Machining: Factory machine doors for non-surface applied hardware per DHI-WDHS-3, hardware schedules, and templates 1.
Transom and Side Panels: Fabricate matching panels with identical construction, surfaces, and finish 1.
Openings: Factory cut and trim openings for lights and louvers 1. Glazing for factory-finished doors must be factory installed per Section 088000 "Glazing" 1.
Exterior Doors: Require water repellent treatment after fabrication and before finishing 1. Out-swinging doors must be flashed with manufacturer's standard metal flashing 1.

2.9 FACTORY FINISHING
All factory finishing must comply with referenced quality standards 1. Fabrication must be completed before finishing 1. All faces, four edges, edges of cutouts, and mortises must be finished, though stains and fillers may be omitted on specific edges and cutouts 1.

PART 3 - EXECUTION
This section outlines the requirements for examination, installation, and adjustment of wood doors 1.
3.1 EXAMINATION
Prior to hanging doors, the Installer, in the presence of the Engineer, must examine doors and installed door frames to verify compliance with type, size, location, swing, and ensure frames have level heads and plumb jambs 1. Doors with defects must be rejected, and unsatisfactory conditions corrected before installation proceeds 1.
3.2 INSTALLATION
Installation must strictly adhere to the manufacturer's written instructions and referenced quality standards 1.

Hardware: Installation details for hardware are specified in Section 087111 "Door Hardware (Descriptive Specification)" 1.
Fire-Rated and Smoke-Control Doors: Must be installed in accordance with NFPA 80 and NFPA 105, respectively 1.
Job-Fitted Doors: Doors must be aligned and fitted in frames with uniform clearances and bevels, without trimming stiles and rails beyond manufacturer's limits or fire-rated door allowances 1. Edges, cutouts, and mortises must be sealed after fitting and machining 1.
Clearances: 3.2 mm at heads, jambs, and between pairs of doors 1. 3.2 mm from bottom of door to top of decorative floor finish, or 6.4 mm to top of threshold 1. Fire-rated doors must comply with NFPA 80 clearances 1.
Bevels: Non-fire-rated doors require a 3-1/2 degree bevel at lock and hinge edges 1. Fire-rated doors require a 3-1/2 degree bevel at the lock edge 1.


Factory-Fitted and Factory-Finished Doors: Factory-fitted doors are to be aligned for uniform clearance 1. Any finish restoration required due to onsite fitting must be completed before installation 1.

3.3 ADJUSTING
After installation, doors must be adjusted to ensure proper operation 1. Doors that do not swing or operate freely must be rehanged or replaced 1. Damaged doors or those not complying with requirements must be replaced; repair or refinishing is only permissible if the work complies with all requirements and shows no evidence of repair 1.
